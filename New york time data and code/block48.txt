Seth Kaye has hot pink hair and Pokémon stuffed animals on his shoulder. He mines Litecoin, a cryptocurrency. He’s a designer. And he is trying to start a solar farm in Washington where he lives. Mr. Kaye was one of the more than 8,400 people in New York this week for Consensus 2018, a blockchain-themed conference where people pitched ideas, mingled and reveled in the possibility of disrupting health care or real estate or how government works.
